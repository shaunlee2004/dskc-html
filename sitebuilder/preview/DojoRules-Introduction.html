Dojo Rules - Introduction

These are rules and regulations for the Dominion Shotokan Karate Club, LLC (DSKC). They have been designed to help
students feel more comfortable from the beginning and instill a basic knowledge of the culture, terminology and procedures
that are required in a dojo. It will require some time and effort during your training to learn the proper procedures, rules of
etiquette and aspects of Japanese culture. This is understood as practicing karate is much different than most endeavors
you have undertaken in your life thus far. You will make mistakes; learn from them. If you have questions, feel free to ask the
sensei or a sempai at the proper time. We are all here to help you in your journey.

We practice the Japanese art of Shotokan Karate and are affiliated with the International Shotokan Karate Federation (ISKF)
headquartered in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. The chairman and chief instructor is Master Teruyuki Okazaki, 10th Dan and a
direct student of the founder of Shotokan, Master Gichin Funakoshi. All students are required to become members of the
ISKF and can participate in all ISKF tournaments, special trainings, Master Camps and Fall Camps. In addition, students
who are traveling should be able to practice at any ISKF dojo, worldwide, for a nominal fee. You will need to see your
instructor prior to your trip to obtain a letter of introduction for the sensei at the other dojo, to discuss the small gift, omiyage,
you should bring to the sensei and about your expected conduct. Remember, you are representing our dojo; follow all their
rules and do your best during your training visit.

“The ultimate aim of karate-do lies not in victory or defeat, but in the perfection of character of the person who studies it.”
Master Gichin Funakoshi
