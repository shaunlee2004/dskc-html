Dojo Rules - Protocol
When the class begins, students will line up facing shomen (the front of the dojo), in musubi dachi, according to rank, with
the higher ranking member to your left. Sensei will take a position in front of the class and sit in zazen (formal seated
position). The ranking student will announce:

  • Seiza!  This is the command to sit in the formal seated position. Students will kneel, in order of rank, until everyone
    is seated. You will be instructed about these procedures on your first day by a senior student.
  • Mokuso! This is a brief period of meditation, self reflection or personal preparation. You close your eyes, breathe in
    slowly through your nose and then exhale slowly through your mouth. Remain still and quiet.
  • Mokuso Yame! Open your eyes and remain quietly seated.
  • Shomen ni Rei! Bow to the front of the dojo. Please note: This has no religious significance. It is only showing
    respect to Masters Funakoshi, Nakayama and Okazaki.
  • Sensei ni Rei! Bow to sensei. You should say, osu!, and maintain your bow until sensei finishes bowing. When you
    rise, you must wait for the person to your left to stand before you stand. Once everyone is standing, you may move to
    your position on the floor.

Once everyone is in position, a sempai will lead the class in warm up. Proper respect and attitude will be expected at all
times. You are in class to train. Interruptions and misbehaving will not be tolerated! It is not only rude and disrespectful, it is
also dangerous. Safety is our first concern. If you need to leave the floor for any reason, you must have sensei’s permission.
There are no breaks for drinks or to use the restroom; do these things before class starts. If it is excessively hot or the
training has been very difficult, sensei may allow a break. If so, be quick and return promptly.

Your karate training will consist mainly of three parts, kihon, kata and kumite. Never ask sensei, “What are we going to do
today?” It is considered rude and impolite to ask. You are there to do whatever you are asked to do. Ideally, we will work on
all aspects of karate in each class; however, this may not always be the case. Be prepared for anything. Have your gloves
and mouth guard with you for each class. Training includes:

  • Kihon - These are the basics of karate. It includes all blocks, strikes, punches, kicks and stances.
  • Kata - The kata, or forms, are a series of blocks, strikes, kicks, punches and stances combined to simulate an
    attack by several opponents. Every kata begins with a block to foster the idea of karate as a method of self-defense,
    not attack.
  • Kumite - This is sparring or fighting. Kumite is geared specifically to each level of training and gets progressively
    more intense as the student increases in rank.

At the end of class, we often have cool-down stretches, partner stretches or, in the case of the children’s classes, “animal
exercises.” These last exercises are done when we have extra time and are designed to build muscle and stamina in the
children, most of whom are too young to be lifting weights. They consist of twelve sets of movements, most named after the
animal they resemble. The group includes: monkey, bear, seal, cricket, duck walk, duck hop, frog, spider, bunny, crab,
elephant, and military crawl. We also do suicide sprints, jogging, and other cardiovascular exercises in the dojo.

The command to line up signals the end of the class. Students are to run and line up in musubi dachi. You will line up in
the same order as you did at the beginning of class. The ranking sempai will announce:

  • Seiza! Sit in the same manner as in the beginning of class.
  • Mokuso! Close your eyes and clear your mind.
  • Mokuso Yame! Open your eyes.
  • Dojo Kun! This is the reciting of the dojo rules aloud. Senior students are expected to know it both in English and
    Japanese. NOTE:  The Dojo Kun will be listed later in these rules.
  • Shomen ni Rei! Bow to the front.
  • Sensei ni Rei! Bow to sensei and say, Domo arigato gozaimashita, or Thank you very much. Sensei will stand and
    signal the sempai to stand. You will wait to stand until the person on your left stands. Everyone then bows again,
    standing, and leaves quickly and quietly.
